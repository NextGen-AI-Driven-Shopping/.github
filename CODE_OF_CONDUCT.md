# Code of Conduct

We want RecommendMe to be a place where people can contribute without friction. That means respectful communication, clear expectations, and code review focused on the work rather than the person.

## Our pledge

We commit to a harassment-free, inclusive experience for everyone involved in the project, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or role.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and direct.
- Keep feedback specific, factual, and actionable.
- Critique the implementation, not the contributor.
- Ask questions when something is unclear instead of guessing.
- Treat mistakes as something to correct, not to weaponize.

## Collaboration standards

- Read the relevant docs before changing code.
- Keep changes inside the agreed scope unless you have approval to go wider.
- Use issues and pull requests to keep discussion visible.
- Make reviewable changes with a clear explanation of the tradeoffs.
- If you discover a problem in another area, raise it early rather than silently working around it.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation
- Deliberately hostile or dismissive communication
- Repeatedly ignoring agreed workflow or review requirements
- Publishing private security issues publicly
- Misrepresenting the impact of a change or hiding known breakage

## Enforcement

Reports can be sent to the project maintainers at `teamlead@gmail.com`.

Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled confidentially to the extent possible. Corrective action may include a warning, temporary removal from review or contribution privileges, or other steps appropriate to the situation.

## Scope

This policy applies in GitHub issues, pull requests, code reviews, commit messages, discussions, and any other project space used for RecommendMe.
